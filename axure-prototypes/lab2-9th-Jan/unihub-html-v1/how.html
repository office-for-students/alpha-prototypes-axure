Finance

Understanding how much it will cost and how you will pay for it may be key to your decision. If you move away, you will need to think about accommodation costs and living costs in addition to tuition fees.Some courses have additional costs for materials or field trips.





There may be more support available than you think and tuition fee loans don’t need to be paid back until you are earning over a certain amount.


You also may be able to access grants or bursaries to help with living costs, childcare or if you have additional needs due to a disability.


Financial arrangements are different across the UK.

Entry requirements

You may be asking “Can I get in?”.

 

This depends on the course you’re applying to, but you can find information on the qualifications and grades you will need on university and college course pages.


Some course providers will give a slightly lower offer to students from backgrounds where there is lower participation in higher education as part of their strategies to widen access, so look out for this if it is likely to apply to you.


If you are a mature student (over 21) you may be able to gain accreditation for prior learning or work experience in place of meeting some of the entry requirements. Discuss this with the provider you are interested in studying with.


You may need to complete an access course prior to entry if

you don’t currently meet entry requirements. Your provider will advise if this would be appropriate and there is financial support available.

How to apply

If you are planning to study full-time, you will need to apply through UCAS. There is detailed guidance available on how the process works on the UCAS website.




If you are intending to study part-time or online (or through another flexible route), you will need to apply through the course provider. Their admissions department can give you advice on how to do this.
