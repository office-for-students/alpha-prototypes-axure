Career prospects

A higher education qualification can open doors for you whether you are a school or college leaver, or already working. Graduates can often choose from a greater range of jobs and are more likely to be employed than non-graduates.


For certain jobs you might need a degree – for some even a degree in a certain subject area.

Earnings

Graduates have greater earning potential that non-graduates and attract higher salaries throughout their working life.


Of course earnings depend on a lot of different things, but in general graduates earn more than non-graduates.


Skills

As well as developing an indepth knowledge of the subject you study, higher education gives you the opportunity to develop the skills that you will need for work or further study, such as

time management, problem solving and team work.


Not only will you develop academic and professional skills, but going to uni can help you become more independent and develop social skills that benefit you for the rest of your life.

Experience

For some people it’s the experience of being at uni that is most rewarding. That could be about academic interest in your subject, socialising and extra-curricular activities like sport,

building your confidence, or the overall satisfaction of developing your skills. You don’t have to live away from home to get these benefits either.
