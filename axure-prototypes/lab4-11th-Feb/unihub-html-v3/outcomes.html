Professional accreditation


If your reason for studying is to get into a particular career, it might be necessary to take a course accredited by a professional body. For example, you need to complete a course accredited by the relevant body to become an architect or a lawyer. In some cases, accreditation just means a course is recognised as meeting employer needs for a particular industry.


Any professional accreditations held by the course are displayed on our course pages.

How to choose a course

« How to choose a course

Higher education can help you get a good job and graduates typically earn more. But that’s not important to everyone when choosing their course.


You might just want to study a subject that you enjoy or are good at or benefit from the other skills studying at a higher level can help you develop.


The reason why you want to study will affect the information that will be important to you when deciding what course to study.


Preparation for employment


Universities and colleges will tell you how they will help you to prepare for a job while you’re studying with them. You can find the link to this information on the specific course page you are interested in under preparation for future employment.


There you’ll find information on:


work placements - which are a great way of preparing for employment. Students who have done a placement as part of their course are more likely to be employed afterwards


careers advice, including one-to-one appointments with experienced advisers


help you’ll get with your CV, job searching, applications and interview preparation


any employability programmes they run

Where can I find this information?


You can sometimes find more in-depth information about what graduates went on to do from the course page on their website.


Why is this information relevant?


If employment outcomes are important to you, understanding how successful students have been finding employment, the type of job they get, and what they earn after attending this course can give an indication of what your outcomes might be.


It’s important to bear in mind that things may change by the time you graduate though.


How well students are supported to find work can be key, which is why you need to consider the information universities and colleges make available about this, and professional recognition of a course can be a real benefit in certain industries. 


Course completion


We also have information on the percentage of students who did not complete their course on each course page. High rates of non-completion can be a cause for concern, and we would recommend you discuss these with the university or college to understand the reason for them if the course is one you are interested in.
