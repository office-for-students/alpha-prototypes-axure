Loans and grants for living costs

You may be able to borrow money to help pay for tuition and living costs. You might also get extra money, which doesn't need to be paid back, based on your circumstances.


This is a general guide as each country has a slightly different way of doing things. For more specific information you should visit your country’s website


These cover the costs of your tuition up to £9,500. 

If your course costs more than that, you’ll have to pay the rest yourself. 


You may qualify for other grants or financial help from your government or from the university or college in the form of a scholarship or bursary.  These will be based on your circumstances and are not always available or guaranteed.

Working out when and how much you’ll repay

Learn about student finance in your country

Also known as maintenance loans and grants. This is a ‘means tested’ loan or grant and is based on your household income.  Which means the higher your household income, the less you’ll receive.


You don't have to pay back a grant unless you’ve been paid too much. This can happen if you leave the course early or drop out.


These are usually awarded to students based on their personal circumstances, or if they come from a low-income family. Bursaries can help students who may face more barriers to attending higher education, enabling them to access higher education.


Ask the university or college you’re interested in about their bursaries and other financial help.

You can find this information by following the student support link on the [course page] you’re interested in. Contact the university or college directly if you’re still not sure if you’re eligible.


A fee waiver is when a university or college pays part, occasionally,  all of a student's tuition fee. The amount you need to borrow as part of your loan will therefore be reduced. This is an option that might be offered to you if you’re on a low income.

You can apply for extra help if you’re on a low income, have children or dependent adults, or have a disability.

You don’t have to start paying your loan back until you earn over a certain amount.
